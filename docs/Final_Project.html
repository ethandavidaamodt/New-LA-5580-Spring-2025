Park Access in Iowa

Analyzing 10-Minute Walk Access by Census Tract

Project Overview

This project explores which communities in Iowa live within a 10-minute walk of a public park, using spatial data to analyze accessibility and equity. The analysis combines ACS census tract data with state park datasets to generate a meaningful visual assessment of park proximity.

Data & Methodology

  • Population data: 2022 ACS 5-Year Census Tract Estimates
  • Park data: Iowa Open Data Portal
  • Software: R (tidycensus, sf), ArcGIS Pro, Tableau
  • Used spatial buffers to estimate 10-minute walk access to parks, based on tract centroids intersecting park service areas.

Key Findings

  • Approximately XX% of tracts have access to a park within a 10-minute walk.
  • Access was lower in rural areas and some lower-income tracts in urban areas.
  • Policy attention may be warranted in tracts with high population but no nearby parks.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights both gaps and opportunities in park accessibility across Iowa. It demonstrates how spatial data can support equitable planning decisions that improve community well-being.

Personal Reflection

Throughout this project, I gained experience working with spatial data, translating raw census data into visual insights, and using Tableau to communicate complex findings. I also deepened my understanding of equity in urban planning, especially how access to green space impacts public health.
