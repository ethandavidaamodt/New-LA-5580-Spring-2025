Park Access in Iowa

Analyzing 10-Minute Walk Access by Census Tract

Project Overview

This project explores public park accessibility across ten counties in Iowa by estimating the percentage of each census tract’s population that lives within a 10-minute walk (approximately 800 meters) of a park. Using a combination of spatial analysis tools—including ArcGIS Pro, R, and Tableau—I created a custom methodology to simulate walking distances, analyze population overlap, and visualize access patterns.

To capture a range of urban and rural conditions, I selected both the five most populous counties and five mid-sized counties in Iowa. Park data was cleaned and filtered by type, then buffered to simulate walkable access zones. These buffers were intersected with census tract boundaries, and population estimates were adjusted using an area-weighted overlay method to avoid overestimation.

The final outputs reveal patterns of accessibility across different communities, highlighting areas where access is limited and offering a foundation for future planning decisions aimed at improving equitable park distribution.
